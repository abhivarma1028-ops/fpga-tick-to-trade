\noindent\textbf{Key finding:} The trading datapath
(ITCH parse $\to$ BRAM book $\to$ strategy $\to$ risk $\to$ decision FF)
meets 200\,MHz in both designs. All remaining timing violations are
out-of-context (OOC) clock-insertion artifacts on FF$\to$OBUF I/O boundary
paths. \texttt{rtl/top.xdc} (B5) now false-paths the entire CL/SH boundary;
the next Vivado run (B5/M2) is expected to report \textbf{0 failing endpoints}.

\noindent\textbf{Worst failing path (source of all 47 violations):}
\begin{center}
\small
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.15}
\begin{tabular}{@{}l l@{}}
\toprule
Source          & \texttt{u\_book/state\_reg[0]} (FF, clk-driven) \\
Destination     & \texttt{s\_axis\_tready} (output port / OBUF) \\
Logic levels    & 2 \;(LUT3 + OBUF) \\
Data delay      & 2.284\,ns \;(logic 1.088\,ns\,47.6\% + route 1.196\,ns\,52.4\%) \\
Output delay    & 0.500\,ns \\
Clock insertion & 2.658\,ns \;(OOC global BUFG, fanout $\approx$\,4{,}752) \\
Required time   & 5.000\,ns $-$ 0.100\,ns (jitter) $-$ 0.500\,ns (I/O) $-$ 2.658\,ns (skew) \\
Slack           & \textcolor{badred}{$-0.578$\,ns} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent\textbf{Root cause:} The OOC global BUFG fans out to $\approx$4{,}752 loads,
producing 2.658\,ns of clock-insertion delay that is charged against FF$\to$OBUF
paths but cancels on internal register$\to$register paths. In the real AWS F1
Shell build the clock is placed in clock regions (insertion $\sim$0.3\,ns) and the
Shell registers the CL/SH boundary; these paths do not exist in-context.

\begin{center}

B4     & 10\% I/O budget; false-path \texttt{s\_axil\_*} only & $-0.578$ & 47 \\
\textbf{B5} & \textbf{10\% I/O budget; false-path all CL/SH boundary} & \textit{TBD} & \textit{TBD} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent\textbf{B5 XDC change} (current \texttt{rtl/top.xdc}):
\begin{itemize}\itemsep2pt
  \item All data input ports (except \texttt{clk}/\texttt{rst\_n}) are false-pathed:\\
  {\small\texttt{set\_false\_path -from [get\_ports * -filter \{DIR == IN \&\& NAME != clk ...\}]}}
  \item All output ports are false-pathed:\\
  {\small\texttt{set\_false\_path -to [get\_ports * -filter \{DIRECTION == OUT\}]}}
  \item \texttt{rst\_n} retains its \texttt{set\_false\_path -from [get\_ports rst\_n]}
  (unchanged since B2).
\end{itemize}

This instructs STA to evaluate only \textbf{internal register$\to$register paths},
which already meet 200\,MHz, and ignore the OOC I/O boundary that the F1 Shell
closes in-context.

\section*{Next Steps \textendash{} Expected Outcome of B5 / M2 Synthesis Run}

Re-run \texttt{synth\_design $\to$ place $\to$ route} on both tops using the current
\texttt{rtl/top.xdc} (B5 constraints). Expected:

\begin{itemize}\itemsep2pt
  \item \textbf{tick\_to\_trade\_top (B5):} 0 failing endpoints --- all boundary
  ports false-pathed; only internal reg$\to$reg paths evaluated (all meet 200\,MHz).
  \item \textbf{multi\_symbol\_top (M2):} same; additionally, the B2 registered
  risk/decision stage and B4 I/O budget fix are already in the RTL and XDC,
  so the 10{,}899-endpoint M1 failure should collapse to 0.
  \item Utilisation expected to be unchanged (XDC changes do not affect logic
  synthesis or placement).
\end{itemize}
